Current Research Areas

Biomechanics

The Response of the Skeleton to Mechanical Load

Our lab investigates how bone cells sense and respond to physical forces to maintain skeletal integrity. By utilizing advanced imaging and mechanical testing, we explore the molecular pathways that translate mechanical signals into new bone formation.

Rare Disease

New Treatments for Osteogenesis Imperfecta

Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI), or "brittle bone disease," is a genetic disorder characterized by bone fragility. Our research focuses on identifying novel pharmacological targets to improve bone quality and reduce fracture risk in OI patients.

Developmental

Skeletal Modeling During Growth

We study the dynamic process of skeletal modeling to understand how bones achieve their final size and strength. This research tracks spatial and temporal changes in bone geometry from early development through adulthood.
